Here's A Timeline Of Tupac Shakur's Short Life:

  • 1971 - Tupac Amaru Shakur is born on June 16th, 1971 in East Harlem, NY.
  • 1990 - Tupac joins rap group Digital Underground as a dancer, backup MC and roadie.
  • 1990 - Tupac makes first rap apperance on Digital Underground's single "Same Song" and releases his first solo album 2Pacalypse now under Interscope Records featuring singles such as "Brenda's Got a Baby" and "Trapped"
  • 1992 - Tupac makes acting debut in movie Juice
  • 1993 - Tupac releases his sophmore project, Strictly 4 MY N.I.G.G.A.Z..., featuring hit singles "Keep Ya Head Up" and "I Get Around." Shakur also stars in John Singleton's film Poetic Justice.
  • 1994 - Tupac stars in Above the Rim with two songs featured in the film titled "Pour Out A Little Liquor" and "Pain." Shakur gets robbed and shot multiple times in the lobby of Quad Recording Studios in Manhattan.
  • 1995 - Tupac serves 9 months in jail until Suge Knight bails him out for 1.4 million in exchange for a 3-record deal with Death Row Records. His third album Me Against The World was released shortly after the his sentence began. In a interview with Vibe magazine he accuses Puff Daddy and The Notorious B.I.G. for the events that happened in the lobby of Quad.
  • 1996 - Tupac releases All Eyez On Me, the double album goes multi-platinum and has two Billboard Hot 100 #1 singles "How Do U Want It" and "California Love." He alongside the Outlawz release "Hit'Em Up" a diss record aimed at The Notorious B.I.G. and the Bad Boy Records crew. On September 7th, Tupac is shot multiple times in a drive-by following a boxing match in Las Vegas and passes away on September 13th. A month after he stars in a film titled Bullet and his fifth solo album, The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory the following month.
